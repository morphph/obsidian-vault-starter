# Source: Ryan Sarver — AI Chief of Staff on OpenClaw

## Summary
Viral X post (217K views, 2.7K bookmarks) by VC [[ryan-sarver|Ryan Sarver]] detailing how he built "Stella," an AI chief of staff on [[OpenClaw]] that manages his fundraise pipeline, meeting prep, task management, relationship tracking, and information filtering. Demonstrates production-grade AI assistant architecture with two-layer memory, kaizen self-improvement, and strict LLM/script separation.

## Key Takeaways
- **Two-layer memory:** Daily markdown logs (auto-generated) + curated MEMORY.md (self-synthesized by the AI). Flat markdown, git-backed, no database — validates [[index-over-rag]] and [[compiler-analogy]] patterns
- **[[kaizen-loop]]:** Friday cron scans community + daily friction detection → Sunday review → continuous improvement. Uniquely AI-native: "A human chief of staff can't scan what hundreds of builders are doing and cross-reference against your system every week"
- **[[llm-judgment-vs-scripts]]:** LLMs handle synthesis/prioritization/drafting; Python handles deterministic operations. "When you push deterministic work through an LLM, things break unpredictably and you stop trusting the system"
- **[[zero-friction-capture]] validated:** Daily notes auto-generated by scripts, meetings auto-processed via Granola API, emails auto-triaged. User does nothing — knowledge flows as side effect of normal work
- **Operational rhythm:** 9am brief, 6pm wrap, silence when nothing to say — "makes it feel like working with a chief of staff rather than using a tool"
- **Fundraise pipeline at scale:** 100+ LP contacts across countries, full relationship context, meeting prep with prior history
