# Steph Zhang

## Summary
**a16z** investor (X: @steph_zhang). First a16z voice in our wiki. Author of "From System of Record to System of Intelligence" (2026-05-14) — a16z's thesis that the next decade of enterprise software value will accumulate in the **reasoning / orchestration layer above** the SoR databases (Salesforce, HubSpot), not in new SoR competitors. **Same story as friend-graph → newsfeed, applied to enterprise GTM.**

## Details

### Single longform in wiki (so far)
**2026-05-14 — From "System of Record" to "System of Intelligence"** ([[source-stephzhang-system-of-intelligence]]). 458K views, 779 bookmarks. Companion piece to a16z's "Is Software Losing Its Head?".

Key arguments:
- CRM ≠ disappearing, but becoming "**just an input**" to a higher orchestration layer (like the friend graph after newsfeed)
- **Old gravity = colocation** (humans look in one place); **new gravity = orchestration** (agents pull from many)
- Switching cost flip: "all our data in Salesforce" → "all our workflows + reasoning + institutional context in our AI layer"
- **Counter-intuitive empirical finding:** CRM usage has *increased* since AI adoption — agents write richer notes, giving reps reason to consult
- "**First prize is a Cadillac. Second prize is a set of steak knives.**" (Old: SoR winners take all; new: SoI winners may take all)
- **Foundation Model is not a GTM application** — the new app layer is the domain-specific orchestration between model and customer

### Why his voice matters
- **a16z = portfolio influence** — they're funding the SoI startups. Their thesis becomes self-fulfilling to some degree.
- **First VC strategic-thesis source in our wiki** balances the heavy builder + engineering coverage. Useful for understanding *what the market is being primed to value*, separate from what's technically interesting.
- **Cross-referenced with our existing concepts** — Steph's "System of Intelligence" maps directly onto Garry Tan's [[filing-cabinet-vs-nervous-system|nervous system]] framing and the [[thin-harness-fat-skills]] architecture, just at enterprise scale rather than personal scale.

### Bias check
- a16z has portfolio bets in the SoI thesis — the framing serves the investment thesis
- "Pie grows, not shrinks" is the same prediction every automation wave makes; sometimes right, sometimes wrong
- Calling Salesforce's decline early has been the historical pattern for VC theses about incumbents — frequently wrong
