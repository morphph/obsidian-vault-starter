# Five Levels of Thinking (and the Lines/Levels/Altitudes framework)

## Summary
[[dan-koe|Dan Koe]]'s creator-economy-friendly packaging of Ken Wilber's Integral Theory: thinking has **width (lines)**, **depth (levels)**, and **height (altitude — average across all your lines)**. Five cognitive levels — Instinctual → Conformist → Individualist → Synthesist → Holist — with **Levels 1-2 = "first tier" (dogmatic)** and **Levels 3-4 = "second tier" (perspectival)**. The framework's load-bearing claim: **"stupid thinking" is reacting with pre-programmed thoughts; "genius thinking" is the ability to keep thinking when your mind feels threatened.**

## Details

### The three dimensions of thinking

| Dimension | What it is | How you grow it |
|-----------|------------|------------------|
| **Lines** (width) | Domains you know about | Learn marketing, history, fitness, philosophy, code — anything specific |
| **Levels** (depth) | How sophisticated your reasoning *about each line* is | Move from "memorized facts" to "models" to "models of models" |
| **Altitude** (height) | The average across all your lines | Develop multiple lines deep, not just one |

### The 5 levels (cognitive development stages)

| Level | Name | Mode of thinking |
|-------|------|------------------|
| **0** | Instinctual | React to stimuli with little thinking. Newborn. |
| **1** | **Conformist** | Black-and-white. Follow rules. **Adopt others' perspective.** Right vs wrong by group consensus. |
| **2** | **Individualist** | Critical thinking emerges. **Build your own perspective.** Reject prior beliefs, find your own answers. |
| **3** | **Synthesist** | See your model as **one among many**. Hold contradictions. Use perspectives as tools rather than law. |
| **4** | **Holist** | **Create original perspectives** that didn't exist before. Come to ideas without outside influence. |

### First tier vs second tier
- **First tier (Levels 1-2):** overly dogmatic. "I'm right, you're wrong." Different flavors of the same disease.
- **Second tier (Levels 3-4):** reject dogma. Recognize truth often lies *between* perspectives, not in any one of them.

> "In meaning/belief: you're indoctrinated into one system → you question and reject it → you start to see truths in all systems → you can create a more expansive one."

**Politics:** most fighting is Level 1 vs Level 2. My group vs yours. Neither side thinks beyond their bubble.

### "Smart but dumb" — the killer diagnosis
> "Horizontally advanced but vertically stuck."

A businessman with all the money but unhappy. A creative with beautiful work that can't make a living. A meathead that can't hold a relationship. **They know a lot in their domain but can't see outside the bubble.** The bubble doesn't provide enough surface area to identify problems that lead to suffering.

### What "stupid thinking" is (vs "genius thinking")

**Stupid thinking:**
- One-dimensional — jam everything into your perspective
- Reductionistic — every problem reduces to your domain
- Tribal — only your group can be right
- Doesn't question — "that's just how it's done"
- **Stops thinking when threatened** — closes the mind once it hits the limit of the known

**Genius thinking:**
> The ability to hold threatening ideas in the realm of possibility, paired with the intention to **understand** rather than just **know**.

> **The mark of genius thinking is the width, depth, and height at which you can think *without being cut off* from venturing further** — often because you held an idea as absolute.

### Knowing vs Understanding (the key distinction)

- **Knowing is horizontal.** Memorize facts about a domain. Becomes competence → "expert."
- **Understanding is vertical.** How sophisticated your entire cognitive OS is. One can understand a lot while knowing very little — and that insight leads to a more worthwhile result.

**The world doesn't need more experts. It needs more understanding.**

### The skill-tree mental model
Higher-level traits unlock only once you've put points into lower-level requirements:
- Can't reach Level 3 of business until you've also developed psychology, fitness, personal development
- "Polymaths win because they have altitude"

### When you hit a wall in your domain
> "You started a business. Learned marketing, sales, product. Made money. Then you hit an invisible roadblock. You can't identify what's wrong, so you blame the market."

**The block isn't in business — it's in the *other* lines you never developed.** Leadership, time management, spiritual wholeness. You can't see the real problem because your bubble doesn't include it.

### Practical guidance

**Reach your highest ability to think by:**
1. **Noticing when your mind feels threatened.** That's the signal.
2. **Being honest with yourself** about the discomfort.
3. **At minimum, staying open to new perspectives** — even if you don't adopt them.

**Don't:** collapse, defend, retreat to identity. The mind wants to feel safe (hunter-gatherer wiring). **The world doesn't reward that anymore.**

### Why identity is the trap
> "Stupid thinking is when you stop thinking. **But what makes a person stop thinking? The answer is identity.**"

Latching onto ideas/beliefs/constructs and making them part of *who you are*:
- Diehard partisan → can never think outside the bubble
- Religion / vocation / "I'm a programmer" / "I'm a designer" → same trap

**Adopting values from a position ≠ making them your identity.** You don't have to feel threatened when someone disagrees.

### Most damning observation in the piece
> "Most people, in today's world, have never thought for themselves a single day in their life:
> - Born into a specific part of the world.
> - That part was at a specific level of development.
> - Told what to believe by parents who hadn't questioned their own beliefs.
> - Those beliefs shaped how far you could think — which probably wasn't very far."

### Where this fits in the wiki

- **[[diarization]]** — "the model reads everything and synthesizes" is the *applied* version of this framework for AI agents. Same goal: combine multiple lines, get to second-tier thinking.
- **[[filing-cabinet-vs-nervous-system]]** — Garry Tan's framing applies to *info systems*; Dan Koe's applies to *thinking systems*. Same evolution (storage → synthesis).
- **[[blog2video]]** + **[[loreai]]** — when generating long-form content, asking "what's the line, the level, and the altitude of this piece?" is a useful editorial check. Most AI-generated content sits at Level 1-2 (regurgitates the consensus). Level 3+ content stands out.
- **[[quality-gate-loop]]** — Khairallah's quality dimensions (voice match / hook / value density / originality) are essentially "is this Level 3+ output, or just Level 1-2 regurgitation?"

### Caveats
- This is **Ken Wilber's Integral Theory repackaged**, not original to Dan Koe. He mentions Wilber once but doesn't cite. Useful to know if you want to dig in.
- The "5D" naming is rhetorical (it's 4D + time). The framework is genuinely 4-quadrant + temporal.
- Developmental psychology has its critics (Wilber especially). Treat as a useful lens, not an empirical truth claim.
