## Article: How to think like a strategic genius (5d thinking)

Your ability to think determines the outcome of your life. That is not an exaggeration.

Especially if you consider yourself a smart person, **because you are the most likely to think like an idiot.**

> "Faith is much better than belief. Belief is when someone else does the thinking." – Buckminster Fuller

## What stupid thinking looks like

If you want to understand what something is, like genius level thinking, then it helps to understand what it is not.

- **Stupid thinking is one-dimensional** — people try to jam everything into their own perspective
- **Stupid thinking is reductionistic** — experts in one domain try to reduce everything to that domain ("just a strategy problem")
- **Stupid thinking is tribal** — you only trust your group, party, or tribe; everyone else is wrong because they don't conform
- **Stupid thinking does not question** — your justification is "that's just how it's done"

**As a whole, stupid thinking is about closing your mind off once you've reached the limits of what you know. Stupid thinking is to stop thinking too early.** To reach a point where you react with pre-programmed thoughts and fail to reach novel insight.

## A definition of genius thinking
> The ability to hold threatening ideas in the realm of possibility, paired with the intention to **understand** rather than just **know**. The mark of genius thinking is illustrated by the **width, depth, and height** at which you can think without being cut off from venturing further, often due to holding an idea as absolute. It's your ability to traverse the full, universal web of ideas and pull them together into something coherent or useful, if not something entirely new.

## Knowing vs Understanding

- **Knowing is horizontal.** You learn a lot and memorize facts about a specific domain until you become competent. People who know a lot are "experts" — but we don't need more experts.
- **Understanding is vertical.** It's about how sophisticated your entire cognitive operating system is. One can understand a lot while knowing very little.

**The "smart but dumb" phenomenon** stems from being **horizontally advanced but vertically stuck**. A businessman with all the money in the world but unhappy. A creative with beautiful work that can't make a living. A meathead that can't hold a relationship. They know a lot in their respective domain but can't see outside their bubble.

Your mind is how you interact with reality. You process information → make sense of it (thinking) → make a choice → receive information as feedback → respond and repeat. **Thinking determines the outcome of your life.**

## Altitudes, levels, and lines of thinking

(References Ken Wilber's developmental framework)

### Lines = horizontal development (width)
Domains you can know a lot about. Marketing, astrophysics, politics, social dynamics, religion. When you learn domain-specific knowledge, you increase the **width** of that line.

### Levels = vertical development (depth)
5 core patterns observed in cognitive development:

- **Level 0 — Instinctual:** Born acting on pure survival; react to stimuli with little thinking
- **Level 1 — Conformist:** Black-and-white. Follow rules, obey authority without questioning. **You adopt others' perspective.**
- **Level 2 — Individualist:** Critical thinking emerges. **You build your own perspective.**
- **Level 3 — Synthesist:** You see your model as one among many. Hold contradictions, use perspectives as tools rather than law.
- **Level 4 — Holist:** Create original perspectives that didn't exist before. Come to ideas without outside influence.

**Levels 1-2 = "first tier"** thinking (overly dogmatic: I'm right, you're wrong).
**Levels 3-4 = "second tier"** thinking (reject dogma, seek truth in the middle).

In meaning/belief: indoctrinated → question and reject prior → see truths in all systems → create a more expansive one.

In politics: most fighting is between Level 1 and Level 2. My group vs yours. Neither side can think beyond their bubble.

**Genius thinking is the ability to continue thinking.** At each level, your space of navigable ideas expands. When you reach Synthesist, you can think far and wide but creating new lines isn't fully developed. Holist unlocks creation.

> **Reach your highest ability to think by: noticing when your mind feels threatened, being honest with yourself, and staying open to new perspectives.**

### Altitudes = cross-dimensional development (height)
The average of all your levels across multiple lines.

Visualize as a skill tree. Higher level traits unlock only after meeting requirements on lower level traits. You can't access Level 3 of dexterity without Level 4 of strength.

**Real-world example:** You started a business. Learned marketing, sales, product. Made money. Hit a wall. Can't identify what's wrong, blame the market. **Your mind is closed; no way forward.** You can't see the actual problem because it could be leadership, time management, or spiritual — but you're too business-minded to entertain that.

**Polymaths win because they have altitude.** You can't reach Level 3 of business until you've "put points" into the other domains that unlock it.

## 4-Dimensional Thinking (the 4 quadrants)

(Ken Wilber AQAL framework, condensed)

4 dimensions of reality = 4 perspectives you can take on any belief, idea, situation, or problem:

|              | Individual                                 | Collective                              |
|--------------|---------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| **Inner**    | Thoughts, emotions, beliefs, consciousness  | Group beliefs, values, ideologies, culture |
| **Outer**    | Visible appearance, behaviors, brain states | Systems, structures, social institutions |

### A worked thinking progression
Problem: "The world is becoming corrupt and meaningless."

- **Collective inner**: How does society control attention and market ideas to the masses?
- **Individual outer**: How can my behavior change to have a positive impact on society? What steps to influence?
- **Collective outer**: What does the current job market look like? Is a job an actual path? What about AI? Can I use it to have impact?
- **Individual inner**: Am I thinking about this the right way? What am I missing, and how does this make me feel?

**Genius thinking is the ability to direct attention in a useful direction.** Gradually zoom out, then re-zoom in from a more enlightened vantage point.

## 5D Thinking: adding TIME / history

Not really a 5th dimension (it's the 4th, time). Applied to all 4 quadrants, your thinking continues to rocket upward.

If you understand history — specifically the **evolutionary patterns** rather than memorized events — you can direct your thinking more accurately.

### 5 things to know about history (a starting point)

#### 1. The master pattern is Transcend and Include
Reality is composed of **whole parts** (wholes that are also parts of a greater whole):
- Word → Sentence → Paragraph
- Machine → Computer → AI
- Human → House → City → State → Country → Continent → Planet → Solar system → Galaxy

Each whole transcends and includes the one before. **Remove the thing that allows another to exist → the chain self-destructs.** (Why environmentalists worry about climate — biosphere collapse takes humans down with it.)

#### 2. How individuals evolve physically
Atoms → Cells → Molecules → Organs → Organism

#### 3. How individuals evolve mentally
- **Egocentric** — care about self and survival
- **Ethnocentric** — care about tribe, group, culture
- **Worldcentric** — care about all groups regardless of differences (but **transcend AND include**: still care about self and tribe — don't sacrifice self because caring about others sounds virtuous)
- **At the most developed levels** — care about reality as the ultimate whole

Most "caring about equality of everyone" arguments fail by skipping the self/tribe layer — using moral high ground self-destructively, which is counterproductive.

#### 4. How societies evolve physically
**Social structures follow technology.**
- Tribes → villages (hoe enabled small-scale farming)
- Villages → empires (horse-drawn plow → food surplus → exploration/conquest)
- Empires → nation-states (machine agriculture)
- Now with computers + AI: ? (left for the reader)

#### 5. How societies evolve mentally
Collectives have identity too. Same egocentric/ethnocentric/worldcentric pattern.

Called **pre-rational, rational, post-rational**: conformity to authority → progress through science (Enlightenment) → integrating the good parts of both.

### How to use this for thinking
- Identify where individuals are when you encounter an idea or conflict. Who do you want as a politician entering the age of AI?
- In debate (political, business, personal), notice the level people are operating from. **Higher-level individuals often need to meet people where they are.**
- Look at the technology of societies and organizations. A business still operating on industrial-era methods (and too dogmatic to adapt) will fail.
- **Locate your own center of gravity.** Reread this letter while thinking about your own development.

## What makes a person stop thinking? Identity.

> "Stupid thinking is when you stop thinking. But what makes a person stop thinking?"
>
> **The answer is identity.**

Latching onto ideas, beliefs, or constructs and making them a part of who you are.

- Diehard republican or democrat → can't think outside that bubble → can't find truth
- Religion, group, vocation — same dynamic
- **Adopting values from a side ≠ making them your identity.** You don't need to feel threatened when someone disagrees.

## How you came to your beliefs (humbling)

Most people have never thought for themselves a single day in their life:
1. Born into a specific part of the world
2. That part was at a specific level of development
3. Told what to believe by parents who hadn't questioned their own
4. Those beliefs shaped how far you could think (probably not very far)

> "Your mind is how you interact with reality. The space in which you can think determines the outcome of your life. If you only think within the confines set by your parents, teachers, employer, and peers on social media, the outcome of your life may not be that pretty."

## Closing

When you feel threatened, don't collapse. Sit with it. Question it. **Your mind wants to feel safe, because that's how it's wired.**

> "It is no measure of health to be well‑adjusted to a profoundly sick society." – Krishnamurti

Our brain is wired like hunter-gatherer ancestors, but we don't live in that world anymore. **The world will only change faster. Those who practice thinking will win.**
