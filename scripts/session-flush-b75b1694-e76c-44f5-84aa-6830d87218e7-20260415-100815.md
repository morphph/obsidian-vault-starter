## 🎓 MODEL LITERACY

**Data Poisoning Attacks**: Imagine a cookbook where someone sneaks in a recipe that calls for bleach instead of lemon juice. Data poisoning works the same way — attackers inject a small number of malicious examples into a model's training data, teaching it to behave incorrectly in specific situations. Today's joint research from Anthropic and UK AISI shows this is more dangerous than previously thought: just a few adversarial documents can compromise models of any size, because the model faithfully learns the poisoned patterns alongside legitimate ones. As more teams fine-tune on scraped web data or user-submitted corpora, understanding poisoning isn't optional — it's table stakes for anyone curating training data.
