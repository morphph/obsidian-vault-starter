## 🎓 MODEL LITERACY

**Model Distillation**: Distillation is training a smaller "student" model to reproduce the outputs of a larger "teacher" model — the student learns to mimic the teacher's behavior at a fraction of the compute cost. It's how most competitive open-source models got good fast: train a smaller architecture on millions of examples generated by frontier models like GPT-4 or Claude. Today's debate between HuggingFace's CEO and Yann LeCun surfaces the core tension — every major lab used distillation to reach the frontier, but now some want to restrict the technique in their terms of service. Understanding distillation explains why open models like Kimi 2.6 are suddenly competitive with closed ones, and why closed labs have a financial incentive to limit who can use the same playbook.
