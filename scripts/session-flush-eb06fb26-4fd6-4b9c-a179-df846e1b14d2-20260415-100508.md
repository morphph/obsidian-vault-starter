## 🎓 MODEL LITERACY

**Data Poisoning Attacks**: Today's Anthropic/AISI research highlights a critical vulnerability — data poisoning, where an attacker injects a small number of malicious documents into a model's training data to introduce specific weaknesses. The key finding: model size doesn't protect you. A few carefully crafted examples can compromise models regardless of their parameter count because the model faithfully learns the patterns in its training data, malicious or not. As more teams fine-tune on scraped web data or user-submitted content, understanding poisoning is essential — the defense isn't a bigger model, it's rigorous data curation, provenance tracking, and adversarial filtering of your training corpus before it ever touches the model.
